Benefits of eating strawberries

The health benefits include reduced cholesterol, blood pressure, inflammation, and oxidative stress. Furthermore, these berries may help prevent big spikes in both blood sugar and insulin levels. Strawberries are an excellent addition to a healthy diet

Nutritional value of strawberry

Strawberries are among the top 20 high-antioxidant fruits and are a good source of manganese and potassium. Just one serving—about eight berries—provides more vitamin C than an orange. Strawberries also have minerals such as calcium, iron, potassium, folate, and magnesium, along with antioxidants called anthocyanins.

Disadvantages strawberry

Strawberries are often heavy in sugar and contain 8.12 mg of sugar per cup. Strawberries may have pesticide residue. Excessive berries consumption might cause stomach distress, heartburn, diarrhea, reflux, bloating, and allergic responses in some .

Facts about strawberries

Rich in antioxidants - strawberries contain the highest amount of one of nature's most important antioxidants - anthocyanins.

Rich in vitamin C, which contributes to strengthening the immune system (about 59 mg per 100 grams of fruit).

Having a low glycemic index - strawberries contribute to lowering blood sugar levels and are suitable for diabetics.

Thanks to the strong concentration of vitamin C in the content of strawberries, they are actually able to eliminate the substance that causes our teeth to turn yellow.

Anti-cancer. The strawberries are rich in ellagic acid, a phytochemical known for its ability to prevent the development of cancer cells.

Rich in fiber and contribute to weight loss thanks to the dietary fiber that contributes to the normal activity of the digestive system and helps lower bad cholesterol and sugar.

The French love their strawberries with wine in general and champagne in particular

In Italy you will be served strawberries with balsamic vinegar and a little black pepper
